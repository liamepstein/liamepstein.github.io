About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence policy and governance. My research focuses on understanding how technological developments in AI can be effectively governed through policy mechanisms, with particular attention to compute governance, export controls, and strategic technology policy.

My approach combines technical understanding of AI systems with policy analysis to develop practical recommendations for institutions seeking to navigate the complex landscape of AI governance.

Research & Work

  • Research Associate at Future Impact Group
  • MPhil in Ethics of AI, University of Cambridge
  • Work on sovereign wealth funds for transformative AI

Current Projects

My current research investigates how compute governance mechanisms can be designed to balance innovation with safety and security concerns. This includes analysis of export control regimes, hardware security measures, and international coordination mechanisms.

Selected Writing

  • "Compute Governance and International Coordination" - Analysis of multilateral approaches to AI governance
  • "Export Controls in the Age of AI" - Policy recommendations for technology transfer restrictions
  • "Sovereign Wealth Funds and AI Investment" - Strategic considerations for public AI investment
